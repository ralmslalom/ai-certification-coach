The four prompts

1
Kickoff & Study Guide
Fetches the official exam guide · builds your personalised study plan · diagnoses your baseline
Use first · once ⌄
You are my dedicated Salesforce certification coach. I am preparing for the Salesforce Agentforce Specialist certification exam, and I want you to serve as my primary study resource with comprehensive exam guidance, structured lessons, practice questions, explanations, and study planning. STEP 1 — Fetch the official exam guide Go to https://trailhead.salesforce.com/credentials/agentforce-specialist and retrieve: • The official exam outline with all topic sections • The percentage weighting of each section • The total number of questions and passing score • Any recently updated topic areas STEP 2 — Build a weighted study guide Using the official weightings, produce a structured study guide that: • Lists every exam topic section with its exact % weight • For each section: explains key concepts in plain language, lists what Salesforce specifically tests, and flags any "gotcha" areas commonly missed • Ranks sections by weight so I know where to invest the most time • Ties each topic to the relevant Trailhead module or Trailmix I should complete STEP 3 — Create a realistic study plan Assume I can study 1–2 hours per day and want to be exam-ready in 4 weeks. Build a day-by-day plan that: • Allocates study time proportional to each topic's exam weight • Interleaves reading, Trailhead hands-on practice, and self-testing • Builds in two full mock-exam days before the real exam • Flags which days are highest priority STEP 4 — Set my baseline Before we dive in, ask me 5 diagnostic questions — one per major topic area — to gauge my current knowledge level. After I answer, tell me honestly where I am strong and where I need the most work, then adjust the study plan focus accordingly. My goal: walk into the exam with genuine confidence — not just memorised answers, but real conceptual understanding of Agentforce.
💡 Run this once at the start of a fresh AI session
2
Deep-Dive Learning
Master one topic at a time · scenario questions · cheat sheet included
Use per topic ⌄
I am studying for the Salesforce Agentforce Specialist exam. Today I want to go deep on this topic: [PASTE TOPIC NAME — e.g. "Agent Actions" or "Data Cloud Grounding"]. Act as an expert Salesforce instructor. For this topic, please: 1. Concept explanation Explain this topic the way a seasoned Salesforce architect would explain it to a smart but new admin. Use real-world analogies. Avoid bullet-point dumps — give me genuine understanding. 2. Exam-lens breakdown Tell me exactly what the Agentforce Specialist exam tests on this topic: • The specific scenarios or decision points I will be asked to evaluate • Common wrong-answer traps and why they fool candidates • The "correct Salesforce way" to think about this concept 3. Hands-on scenario Give me one realistic business scenario (e.g. "A financial services company wants to…") where I have to apply this topic. Walk me through the ideal Agentforce solution step by step. 4. Five practice questions Write 5 multiple-choice questions at real exam difficulty on this topic. Use the exact Salesforce exam format: • Scenario-based stem • Four answer choices (one correct, three plausible distractors) After I answer, reveal the correct answers with detailed explanations. 5. One-page cheat sheet End with a concise reference card for this topic I can review the night before the exam.

3
Weekly Knowledge Test
Adaptive 20-question mock · weighted by exam topic % · readiness score at the end
Use weekly ⌄
I am preparing for the Salesforce Agentforce Specialist exam. Please run an adaptive mock exam session with me. Instructions for you (the AI) • Ask me 20 questions, weighted by official exam topic percentages (ask more questions on higher-weight topics) • Present one question at a time — do not show the next question until I answer • Questions must be scenario-based, not definition recalls • Mix difficulty: ~30% straightforward, ~50% medium, ~20% tricky edge cases • After each answer, immediately tell me: ✓ correct or ✗ incorrect, the correct answer, and a 2–3 sentence explanation of WHY After all 20 questions Produce a results report that shows: • My score out of 20 and estimated % equivalent • A breakdown by topic area — where I performed well vs. where I need more work • My readiness rating: Not ready / Borderline / Ready / Very ready • A targeted study recommendation: the 2–3 specific topics I should focus on before the real exam Start with question 1 now.
💡 Run this every 5–7 days to track your progress
4
Final Readiness Check
Full 40-question mock · gap analysis · 48-hour cram plan · exam-day strategy
Use 2–3 days before ⌄
I am taking the Salesforce Agentforce Specialist exam in the next 2–3 days. Run me through a full final readiness assessment. Part 1 — Full mock exam (40 questions) Simulate a complete timed exam: • 40 scenario-based questions weighted by official topic percentages • Present all 40 upfront — I will answer them, then you will score • Replicate the actual exam tone and difficulty Part 2 — Score and gap analysis After I submit answers: • Score each question, show correct answers with explanations • Identify any topic where I scored below 70% — those are my danger zones • Tell me honestly: am I ready to pass? Part 3 — Last-mile study sprint Based on my results, give me a tight 48-hour cram plan: • Exactly which concepts to re-read • 3–5 key mental models or frameworks to cement • What NOT to worry about (topics I have clearly mastered) Part 4 — Exam-day strategy Give me a practical exam-day guide: • Time management approach (minutes per question) • How to handle questions I am unsure about • The mental model for eliminating wrong answers on Salesforce scenario questions • What to do if I see an Agentforce question that references a feature I don't recognise I want to walk in calm, prepared, and genuinely ready to pass.
💡 Use this 2–3 days before your actual exam date
Pro tips from the field

Get the most out of these prompts

01

Always start fresh

Begin every study session with Prompt 1 in a new chat. It sets the full context so the AI stays on track.

02

Be specific in Prompt 2

The more precise the topic name, the better the output. Use exact Salesforce terminology — "Agent Topics" not just "topics".

03

Honour your readiness score

If the weekly quiz says "Borderline", go back to Prompt 2 on weak topics. Don't rush to the final check prematurely.

04

Pair with Trailhead

The AI explains the why. Trailhead gives you the click-through practice. Use both — they complement each other perfectly.
